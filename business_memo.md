# Business Memo

## Executive Summary

This memo summarizes key customer churn-risk observations identified during the exploratory data analysis (EDA) of the D2C customer dataset. The analysis examined customer orders, product categories, ratings, delivery performance, returns, and support ticket behavior to identify potential factors contributing to customer churn.

## Key Churn Risks

### Risk 1: Negative Customer Sentiment
Support ticket analysis shows the presence of negative sentiment scores, indicating that some customers are dissatisfied with their experience. These customers may be more likely to churn if their issues remain unresolved.

### Risk 2: Delivery-Related Issues
Late deliveries appear among the most common customer support concerns. Poor delivery experiences can reduce customer satisfaction and negatively impact retention.

### Risk 3: Product Returns
Although the return rate is relatively low, returned orders indicate unmet customer expectations. Customers who return products may have a higher risk of future churn.

### Risk 4: Support Ticket Reopenings
A small number of support tickets were reopened after resolution. Reopened tickets may signal unresolved customer concerns and reduced customer trust.

### Risk 5: Category Revenue Concentration
Revenue is concentrated in a few product categories, especially Skin Care. Any decline in customer engagement within these categories could significantly affect overall business performance.

## Recommended Actions

1. Monitor customers with negative sentiment scores and provide proactive customer support.
2. Improve delivery operations and communicate delays transparently to customers.
3. Analyze returned orders to identify common product or service issues.
4. Prioritize customers with reopened support tickets for follow-up actions.
5. Develop targeted retention campaigns for high-value customers and key product categories.

## Conclusion

The exploratory analysis suggests that customer sentiment, delivery performance, support interactions, and return behavior are important indicators of potential churn risk. Addressing these issues proactively can improve customer satisfaction, strengthen retention, and support long-term business growth. These findings provide a strong foundation for the next phase of churn prediction modeling and retention strategy development.
